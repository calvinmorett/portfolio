Organize Internal Communications with a Fluid Design System

Northeast Campaign's inconsistent content strategy confused both creators and potential customers.

A catering company that began as an event planning business, driven by a fascinating purpose to honor those lost in foreign wars and create job opportunities for US and Canadian veterans through meticulously crafted events. As they organized experiences they were also building a vibrant online presence, establishing strong connections with the communities they served.

The company headquarters and primary event space is situated along the northeast border of US and Canada. They started with a small facility that grew to a 3,500-square-foot, dining and event space. Featuring a state of the art kitchen as their hub for culinary innovation, recipe research, and the provision of their signature dishes, with options to be savored at events or taken away in convenient self-service packaging.

With 50 veterans as employees between both countries, who speak a multitude of languages, the variety of diverse experiences and characters built a strong team. However, it also resulted in a strategy issue. Over time, employees began developing their own communication systems, tailored to serve their immediate needs but they weren't necessarily aligned with the brands long-term goals.

Without a centralized system to effectively communicate all their activities, the internal and eventually the client-facing Content Management System fell short of providing the necessary structure to serve both their customers and content creators. As a result, Northeast Campaign needed to rethink their content creation system, seeking to balance something uniform enough to let users easily identify content that was interesting to them, without hindering their authors' ability to be expressive and create what they were passionate about.

User-centered design for diverse audiences. Built up from an MVP into a tool to serve clients and staff.

In the realm of internal networks, intranets have typically struggled to maintain relevancy, often succumbing to abandonment and outdated information, burdening organizations with the need for continuous upkeep. To counter this pattern, Northeast Campaigns took a different approach by developing a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that would generate initial enthusiasm and lay the foundation for process control and ongoing enhancements.

Breaking away from the conventional formula of incorporating various widgets like message boards and weather updates, the initial release of this platform focused solely on delivering relevant news. Recognizing its potential to foster effective communication and cultivate a shared organizational culture, Northeast Campaigns leveraged news as a powerful tool for connecting their diverse workforce across different countries.

The MVP provided an opportunity for user testing and validation, allowing Northeast Campaigns to gather valuable insights and identify areas for further improvement. Throughout the process, the primary goal was to enhance communication and engagement, steering clear of creating another repository of outdated information.

Bringing communication together under a single centralized theme.

By embracing Atomic design as the fundamental principle for platform architecture, we established a system that enables the construction of pages through a collection of components. Introducing the concept of callouts, which resemble cards, we provided users with swift access to desired content.

Within this system, multiple components can seamlessly integrate with one another, with the design of each component tailored to its specific context. When placed within a callout, a component typically showcases condensed snippets of content, whereas a component nested within a full page offers more comprehensive and detailed information.

A brand that travels
in a new direction.

Introducing a brand that signals a new direction, the platform was aptly named "Nexus" as this choice of name reflected the platform's core objective of becoming the central hub for all of Northeast's operations. No longer would multiple content creation groups with competing visions be the norm; now all of NorthEast can build upon the Nexus branding system.

The branding aimed to be a new start for Northeast. By opting for a style that was a step away from their external facing brand, Nexus established itself as a standalone product with its own unique identity.

After unifying their systems under Nexus, Northeast Campaign achieved significant cost savings, solely in server expenses. This consolidation proved to be an instant improvement, with Nexus benefiting all internal staff, who embraced the platform's capabilities. Since everyone is now using the same tool, it allows for a cohesive way for content to be created without adding more complexity, so both the users, content authors and internal staff can succeed.
